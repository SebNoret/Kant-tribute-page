Presentation

Immanuel Kant was a German philosopher and one of the central Enlightenment thinkers. Kant's comprehensive and systematic works in epistemology, metaphysics, ethics, and aesthetics have made him one of the most influential figures in modern Western philosophy.

his life

  • 1724 - Born in Königsberg in Prussia
  • 1740 - Enrolled in the University of Königsberg
  • 1746 - His father dies, forcing Kant to interrup his studies
  • 1755 - Becomes a private tutor in the towns surrounding Königsberg but continued his scholarly research
  • 1766 - become assistant librerian in the Imperial Court Library
  • 1770 - appointed Full Professor of Logic and Metaphysics (Professor Ordinarius der Logic und Metaphysic) at the University of Königsberg
  • 1781 - First edition of the Critique of Pure Reason
  • 1786 - become member of Prussian Academy of Sciences of Berlin
  • 1788 - Edition of the Critique of Practical Reason
  • 1804 - Dies in Königsberg at the age of 79
